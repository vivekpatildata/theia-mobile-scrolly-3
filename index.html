CHAPTER 1 BALTIC ANCHORAGE

146 Days at Anchor

AKADEMIK GUBKIN sat idle at different anchorage locations in the Laconian Gulf ,from April 5 through August 29. The prolonged wait followed its second Cuba delivery of 2025, a pattern suggesting difficulties securing buyers or coordinating payment under sanctions. The vessel's inactivity concluded on August 31 when it sailed for Ust-Luga, a primary Russian oil export terminal in the Baltic Sea.

CHAPTER 4 NORTH SEA

27-Hour Drift

At 11:15 UTC on September 7, speed dropped dramatically to 0.5-1 knot. For the next 27 hours, AKADEMIK GUBKIN drifted with currents, likely awaiting final instructions before entering the Atlantic. At 14:30 UTC on September 8, speed began increasing again. By 15:00 UTC, the ship had returned to normal running speeds and headed south-west through the Atlantic.

KEY FINDINGS

Analysis Summary

  • AKADEMIK GUBKIN only completed two trips so far in 2025, suggesting a difficulty in securing a buyer. Both of the trips have been to Cuba.
  • All vessels involved in the transaction are under US sanctions. Despite this, only EQUALITY attempted to conceal the operation by stopping its AIS broadcast between October 3rd and 5th. This limited use of deceptive practices suggests that both Russia and Cuba are largely unconcerned about hiding their activities, demonstrating persistence of supply chains despite multilateral sanctions.
